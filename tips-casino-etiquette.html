Casino Etiquette for First-Time Visitors

Walking into a Las Vegas casino for the first time can be overwhelming. Whether you’re playing at the tables or the slots, knowing casino etiquette helps you fit in, avoid mistakes, and have a great time.

1. Know the Rules Before You Sit Down

Before joining a game, take a moment to observe and learn the basics.

  • Table minimums vary – Always check before placing your bet.

  • Don’t touch your cards – In games like blackjack, dealers handle the cards in shoe-dealt games.

  • Follow hand signals – Gesturing for hits and stands avoids confusion.

2. Tip the Dealers & Servers

Tipping is a standard part of casino culture.

  • Dealers – If you’re winning, tipping the dealer is appreciated (typically $5 chips here and there).

  • Cocktail servers – Drinks are free while you play, but tipping $1–$2 per drink keeps the service coming.

3. Don’t Hog the Slots or Tables

If you’re taking a break, don’t leave personal items at a slot machine or keep an empty seat at a busy table. Be mindful of other players.

4. Handle Chips and Money Properly

  • Don’t hand money to the dealer – Place it on the table instead.

  • Wait for change – Dealers exchange cash for chips after verifying the amount.

5. Have Fun, But Play Responsibly

Gambling is meant to be entertaining, so set a budget and know when to walk away

Mastering these etiquette tips will help you blend in, avoid rookie mistakes, and enjoy the casino experience to the fullest.
