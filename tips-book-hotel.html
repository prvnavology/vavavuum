Vegas Travel Tips

Best Time to Book Hotels in Las Vegas

Las Vegas is a year-round destination, but timing your hotel booking right can save you money and secure the best accommodations. Whether you’re planning in advance or hoping for a last-minute deal, knowing when to book can make a big difference.

Peak vs. Off-Peak Seasons

Las Vegas experiences major price fluctuations based on demand.

  • High season (March–May, September–November) – Spring and fall bring ideal weather and a packed event calendar, making these the busiest (and most expensive) times for hotels.

  • Low season (June–August, December–February) – Summer temperatures soar, and post-holiday months see fewer visitors, leading to lower hotel rates.

Best Time to Book in Advance

If you’re visiting during a major event or holiday, booking ahead is essential.

  • Conventions and sporting events – Prices surge during CES (January), major boxing matches, and Formula 1 weekends. Book 3–6 months in advance.

  • Holidays and big weekends – New Year’s Eve, Memorial Day, and Fourth of July attract massive crowds. Early booking secures the best rates.

Last-Minute Deals

For flexible travelers, last-minute discounts can be a way to score cheap rates

  • Weekday stays (Sunday–Thursday) – Las Vegas hotels slash prices midweek.

  • Same-day booking apps – Platforms like HotelTonight often offer steep discounts for same-day bookings.

Pro Tip

Compare direct booking prices with travel sites and consider signing up for hotel loyalty programs to unlock exclusive discounts.
